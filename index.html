Banana Cue
Ingredients
  • 4-5 saba bananas (or plantains as a substitute)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
Instructions
  1. Peel the saba bananas and cut each one in half lengthwise.
  2. Heat the oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  3. Roll the bananas in brown sugar until evenly coated.
  4. Fry the bananas until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes.
  5. Remove from the oil and let drain on paper towels before serving.
Pancit Canton
Ingredients
  • 1 pack pancit canton noodles
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast (diced)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 carrot (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions
  1. Cook the pancit canton noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat oil over medium heat and sauté the garlic and onion.
  3. Add the chicken and shrimp, cooking until the chicken is browned and the shrimp turns pink.
  4. Add the carrots, snow peas, and soy sauce, then stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the chicken broth and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the cooked noodles and toss until everything is well combined. Serve hot.
Kare-Kare
Ingredients
  • 1 lb oxtail (cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 lb tripes (optional)
  • 1 bunch bok choy
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 banana blossom (optional)
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp ground rice (to thicken)
  • 2 tbsp annatto oil (for color)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions
  1. In a large pot, simmer oxtail and tripes in beef broth for 1-2 hours until tender.
  2. Add the peanut butter and ground rice, stirring until the sauce thickens.
  3. Add the vegetables and cook for another 10 minutes until tender.
  4. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot with bagoong on the side.
Adobo
Ingredients
  • 1 lb chicken (or pork, cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • Salt to taste
Instructions
  1. Marinate the meat in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat and sauté the onions and garlic until soft.
  3. Add the marinated meat (without the marinade) and cook until browned.
  4. Pour in the marinade and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the meat is tender.
  5. Season with salt to taste and serve with steamed rice.
Lechon
Ingredients
  • 1 whole pig (or pork belly)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup garlic (minced)
  • 2 onions (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 2 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
Instructions
  1. Clean the pig and rub it inside and out with salt and pepper.
  2. Stuff the pig with garlic, onions, bay leaves, and soy sauce.
  3. Roast the pig in a preheated oven or on a spit, basting it with oil and vinegar every 30 minutes.
  4. Roast for 4-6 hours until the skin is crispy and golden.
  5. Serve with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi juice.
Lumpia
Ingredients
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 lb shrimp (peeled and chopped)
  • 1 carrot (julienned)
  • 1/4 cup green onions (chopped)
  • 1 pack lumpia wrappers
  • 1 egg (beaten, for sealing)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
Instructions
  1. Mix ground pork, shrimp, carrots, and green onions in a bowl.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the mixture on a lumpia wrapper, then roll it up tightly, sealing the edges with beaten egg.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and fry the lumpia until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve with a sweet chili dipping sauce.
Sinigang
Ingredients
  • 1 lb pork belly (or shrimp or fish)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 cups tamarind paste or fresh tamarind pods
  • Fish sauce, salt, pepper to taste
Instructions
  1. Simmer pork in water with onion and tomatoes until tender, about 1 hour.
  2. Add the tamarind paste or fresh tamarind to make the soup sour.
  3. Add the vegetables and cook for another 10 minutes.
  4. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot with rice.
Bicol Express
Ingredients
  • 1 lb pork belly (or shrimp)
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 3-4 Thai chili peppers (sliced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions
  1. In a pan, sauté garlic and onion until soft.
  2. Add the pork and cook until browned.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk, chili peppers, and fish sauce.
  4. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve with rice.
Halo-Halo
Ingredients
  • 1 cup crushed ice
  • 1/4 cup sweetened red beans
  • 1/4 cup sweetened jackfruit
  • 1/4 cup leche flan
  • 1/4 cup ube (purple yam) jam
  • 1/4 cup coconut strings
  • 1 scoop ice cream (preferably ube or leche flan flavor)
  • Sweetened corn kernels (optional)
Instructions
  1. Layer the sweetened beans, jackfruit, coconut strings, and ube jam in a tall glass.
  2. Add the crushed ice on top.
  3. Add leche flan and a scoop of ice cream on top of the ice.
  4. Optionally, drizzle with syrup or condensed milk and mix before eating.

About Karinderya ni Edgar

Welcome to Karinderya ni Edgar, your go-to place for savoring the rich and authentic flavors of Filipino cuisine. Founded in 1995 by Edgar Santos, a passionate cook deeply rooted in the culinary traditions of the Philippines, our mission is to bring the warmth of Filipino hospitality and the joy of sharing meals to your table.

From humble beginnings as a small, family-owned eatery in Manila, Karinderya ni Edgar has grown into a cherished spot where friends and families gather to enjoy bold flavors and time-honored recipes. Each dish, from the iconic adobo to the sizzling sisig, is crafted with fresh, locally-sourced ingredients and prepared with love and care.

What We Offer:
  • A wide variety of authentic Filipino dishes.
  • Step-by-step instructions to recreate traditional recipes at home.
  • Tips and tricks to master Filipino cooking.
Our Mission:

To celebrate Filipino culture through food, bringing families and communities together, one flavorful dish at a time.

Join us in experiencing the best of Filipino dining. Whether you're a seasoned food lover or new to Filipino cuisine, Karinderya ni Edgar is here to inspire and delight your taste buds.
